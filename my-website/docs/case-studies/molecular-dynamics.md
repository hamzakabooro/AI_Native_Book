# Case Study: Molecular Dynamics

This case study explores the application of Physical AI Engineering to molecular dynamics simulations, where the goal is to model the physical movements of atoms and molecules over time, respecting quantum and classical physical laws.

## Problem Overview

Molecular dynamics involves simulating the motion of particles governed by interatomic forces. This case study focuses on:
- Learning interatomic potentials from quantum mechanical calculations
- Accelerating MD simulations with neural networks
- Coarse-graining molecular systems
- Predicting material properties from microscale dynamics
- Incorporating quantum effects in classical MD

## Methodology

We'll examine various approaches:
1. Neural network potentials (e.g., SchNet, DeepMD, PhysNet)
2. Message passing neural networks for atomic systems
3. Equivariant networks for molecular properties
4. Learning coarse-grained force fields
5. Transition path sampling with neural networks

## Key Learning Points

This case study highlights:
- How to respect fundamental physical symmetries in molecular models
- Techniques for learning efficient surrogate models
- Approaches to balance accuracy with computational cost
- Methods for incorporating quantum effects in classical models

## Learning Objectives

After completing this case study, you will:
- Understand how to apply Physical AI to molecular systems
- Know techniques for learning interatomic potentials
- Appreciate the challenges in multi-scale materials modeling
- Understand how to preserve physical properties in molecular simulations
